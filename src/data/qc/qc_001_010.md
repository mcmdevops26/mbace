## scenario_006: FLAG
**Issues:**
1. **Key term factual inaccuracy — FDA generic labeling.** The keyTerm "FDA Generic Labeling Requirement" states: "The FDA requires generic medications to clearly state they are equivalent to the branded drug — the 'compare to Advil' label on generic ibuprofen is a regulatory requirement, not a voluntary retail choice." This is misleading. The FDA mandates bioequivalence standards for generic drugs, but the "compare to Advil" shelf labeling found in CVS/Walgreens/Walmart is a retailer marketing practice — it is not an FDA-mandated label format on the drug package itself. The FDA requires generic drug labeling to list the active ingredient (ibuprofen) and indicate it is a generic version, but the specific "compare to Advil" language on store shelves is driven by retailers, not the FDA. This matters because the scenario's entire strategic logic rests on this being an undefeatable permanent constraint — if it is actually a retail practice, Heinz could theoretically negotiate it away.
